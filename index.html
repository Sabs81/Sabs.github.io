NYC is Spending More Than $35 Billion on Active New School Projects Currently Under Construction.

By Sabir Hasko

December 19, 2023

New York City is currently involved in the construction of over 1000 locations for completely new schools, addition to existing schools, lease, and demolition, for a cost of more than $35 Billion at an average of $32 million per project.

According to data issued by New York Cityâ€™s School Construction Authority, they are currently involved in construction at 1078 locations for a cost of over $35 billion. 47% of that is for completely new schools to accommodate demographic shifts in the city. 44% is dedicated to additions to existing school buildings to make space for students. 8% will be used to lease existing buildings and then used as schools, and finally, 1% is dedicated to the cost of demolition of existing structures to make way for new projects.

Nearly half of all new school construction projects are in Queens, where New York City is planning to spend 47% of all funds. Brooklyn will receive 24% of the funds. The Bronx is third at 16%. Staten Island will receive 8% and finally, Manhattan will only get 5%, which is not surprising as it is the most developed out of all the boroughs and practically has very little space for new people and their accompanying school needs.

The School Construction Authority spends funds through other projects that are not included in this assessment.
